FALL 2014

 

Ling 321: Bilingualism

This course examines the cognitive, neurobiological, and linguistic aspects of bilingualism. We will examine topics such as the acquisition of two or more languages by children and adults, the brain mechanisms involved in bilingual language processing, how bilinguals control what linguistic system they use to guide processing, and the consequences of bilingualism for other aspects of cognition.

WINTER 2015

 

Ling 250: Sound Patterns in Human Language

This course is an introduction to the sound structure of language. Students will learn to describe speech sounds in the three domains of speech: the articulatory domain (how speech sounds are produced in the human vocal tract), the acoustic domain (their form in the acoustic medium), and the perceptual domain (how listeners process the incoming speech signal). We will examine the ways in which speech sounds pattern in language to convey meaning, and the ways in which these patterns are similar and different across the languages of the world. Finally, we will discuss current speech technology as it applies to computerized speech synthesis and speech recognition.
